Báo cáo mời

Đinh Nho Hào
Some Inverse Problems in Diffusion Processes

We present several inverse problems for diffusion processes, including the identification of boundary conditions, sources, and coefficients. These problems are well known to be ill-posed. We establish stability results for them and study regularization methods such as Tikhonov regularization and mollification techniques. Numerical results are also presented to illustrate the effectiveness of these methods.

Trương Vĩnh Lân
From Markov Data to Transformers: New Generalization Theory for Modern Deep Learning

Why do highly over-parameterized deep neural networks and Large Language Models (LLMs) generalize so effectively? This question remains one of the central challenges in modern machine learning. In this talk, I present recent theoretical advances that provide new insights into the generalization behavior of modern deep learning systems.

The talk is organized around three themes. First, I discuss learning from dependent data and present generalization bounds for deep neural networks trained on Markovian datasets, revealing how temporal dependence and mixing properties affect learning performance. Second, I introduce new complexity bounds for convolutional neural networks that lead to non-vacuous generalization guarantees and, in some settings, eliminate the dependence on network depth. Finally, I present new rank-dependent generalization bounds for Transformer architectures, showing that low-rank structures can yield sharper guarantees than existing sequence-length-based analyses.

Together, these results provide a unified mathematical perspective on how data dependence, architectural structure, and matrix geometry contribute to the remarkable generalization capabilities of modern deep learning and large language models.

Toshiharu Sato
Physical AI. How It Works for Actual Life/Works

Physical AI is transforming machines from systems that merely “move” into systems that can truly “work” in the real world. This lecture explores how contact control, sensorless intelligence, and autonomous robotics will reshape infrastructure, logistics, agriculture, and daily life, creating the next generation of human–machine collaboration.
